Summary

For this project, I implemented a complete 3D maze game using SDL3 and OpenGL. The environment includes textured walls and floors, colored keys and doors, jumping, animated door opening, power-ups, and a moving Kodama character. The maze is fully navigable with smooth keyboard controls, collision detection, and realistic jumping using gravity and vertical velocity.

I loaded two OBJ models: a tree placed near the goal and a Kodama NPC that moves left and right in front of Door A. If the player collides with the Kodama, the game resets and prints a game-over message. I also added two rotating power-up items: the green one grants speed boost, and the orange one gives higher jump.

Each door type uses a different shape, and doors slide smoothly to the right when unlocked. I used separate textures for the walls and floors, and each key matches its door color. I also recorded two full gameplay videos (test0 and test1) which clearly demonstrate all features better than screenshots.

I attempted point lights above doors, but the fixed-pipeline lighting behaved inconsistently when the camera moved, so I could not fully finish that part. Mouse controls and bump mapping were also attempted but not completed due to time and pipeline limitations.

Extra credit features I completed include:
– Texture-mapped walls/floors
– Smooth realistic jumping
– Moving NPC (Kodama)
– Character interaction (lose on collision)
– Power-ups altering abilities
– Animated sliding doors
– Loading multiple OBJ models
– Full gameplay videos showing all features
