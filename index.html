3D Maze Game

SDL3 + OpenGL Final Project

Real-time textured maze with jumping, keys/doors, NPC, and power-ups

Project Summary

Fully navigable 3D maze with interaction

This project implements a complete 3D maze game using SDL3 and OpenGL. The maze features textured walls and floors, colored keys and doors, smooth keyboard movement, collision detection, and realistic jumping based on gravity and vertical velocity.

Two OBJ models are loaded: a tree placed near the goal, and a Kodama NPC that moves left and right in front of Door A. If the player collides with the Kodama, the game resets and a game-over message is printed. I also created two rotating power-up items: the green power-up grants a speed boost, and the orange power-up increases jump height.

Each door type has a distinct shape, and doors slide smoothly to the right when unlocked. Walls and floors use separate textures, and each key matches the color of its corresponding door. Two full gameplay videos (test0 and test1) demonstrate all features more clearly than static screenshots.

I experimented with fixed-pipeline point lights above doors, but the lighting behaved inconsistently when the camera moved, so that feature is only partially working. Mouse controls and bump mapping were also attempted but not completed due to time and pipeline limitations.

How to Play

Basic controls and gameplay goals.

W / S Move forward / backward
A / D Strafe left / right (or rotate, depending on setup)
Space Jump (with gravity & vertical velocity)
E (or key) Pick up colored key
E near door Unlock matching door and trigger slide animation
ESC Quit game
  • Collect colored keys that match the colored doors.
  • Avoid the moving Kodama in front of Door A – collision causes immediate game over and reset.
  • Touch the green power-up for speed boost, and the orange power-up for higher jumps.
  • Navigate the maze, pass through all required doors, and reach the goal near the tree model.

Graphics & Engine Details

The game is built on top of SDL3 and the fixed-function OpenGL pipeline. The camera and player motion are updated every frame, and the maze geometry is rendered with distinct textured walls and floors. Collision detection prevents the player from walking through walls or doors that are still locked.

  • Textured walls and floors using separate texture maps.
  • Color-coded keys and doors, with matching door unlocking logic.
  • Each door uses a different mesh shape and slides smoothly to the right along its local axis.
  • Jumping uses a vertical velocity + gravity update each frame for smooth arcs.
  • Power-ups are transformed over time to slowly rotate, making them visually stand out.

Two OBJ models are loaded and rendered: a tree near the goal area and a Kodama NPC that patrols in front of Door A by moving left and right between two points.
